Prompt Library

These prompts are ready to copy and paste into Claude or ChatGPT. Replace the bracketed sections with your own details. Each one includes a brief note on what to expect and what to watch for.

Household Admin

1. Explain an official letter or notice

"I have uploaded [a body corporate letter / a council notice / an insurance renewal / a utility contract]. Summarise it in plain English. Tell me: (1) what the main point is, (2) what it will cost me, (3) what I need to do and by when, and (4) anything unusual or concerning."

Expect: A clear, jargon-free summary. Works with uploaded PDFs, photos of paper documents, or pasted text. Watch for: AI occasionally misreads dollar amounts or dates from photographed documents. Double-check any numbers it quotes.

2. Prepare for a phone call

"I need to call [company/organisation] about [issue]. Here is the situation: [describe what happened, what you want, and what they have said so far]. Help me prepare: the key points I should make, the specific questions to ask, what to say if they refuse, and who to escalate to."

Expect: A structured set of talking points you can keep on screen during the call. Watch for: AI will sometimes suggest asking for a specific ombudsman or regulator. Verify the name is correct before citing it on the phone.

3. Understand a contract or agreement

"I have uploaded [a rental lease / a phone contract / a gym membership agreement]. Highlight any clauses that are unusual, one-sided, or that I should negotiate before signing. Tell me the notice period for cancellation and any automatic renewal terms."

Expect: A plain-English breakdown of the terms that matter, especially exit clauses and hidden costs. Watch for: Privacy. If the contract contains personal information, use a paid tier.

Family and Relationships

4. Draft a sensitive message

"I need to write a [text message / email] to [relationship, e.g. my elderly father / my adult daughter / my partner]. The situation is: [describe what you need to say and why it is difficult]. Tone should be [loving but firm / gentle / direct but not aggressive]. Keep it under [150 / 200 / 300] words. Do not use phrases like [list anything that sounds wrong for this relationship]. Make it sound like me, not like a counsellor."

Expect: A draft that gets the structure and tone right. You will want to adjust specific phrases to sound more like you. Watch for: AI tends toward the diplomatic. If you need to be firmer than the draft, say so and iterate.

5. Prepare for a difficult family conversation

"I need to have a conversation with [who] about [topic, e.g. care arrangements for a parent / money / boundaries]. Here is the situation: [describe the dynamics, the history, and what you want to achieve]. Help me prepare: (1) how to open without putting anyone on the defensive, (2) the key points to cover, (3) how to handle the most likely pushback, and (4) how to end with a clear next step."

Expect: A structured preparation that mirrors the workplace difficult conversation example but for personal relationships. Watch for: AI does not know your family. Add the specific details and emotional nuances that only you understand.

6. Help with homework (without doing it)

"My [child/grandchild, age] is struggling with [subject and specific topic]. I do not want you to give me the answer. Instead, explain the concept in a way that would make sense to a [age]-year-old, give me a real-world analogy, and suggest three questions I can ask them that will guide them toward the answer."

Expect: An age-appropriate explanation and a set of leading questions. Watch for: Nothing specific. This is one of the safest and most rewarding uses of AI.

Money and Budgeting

7. Analyse spending patterns

"I have uploaded [three months of bank statements as CSV / a spreadsheet of my expenses]. Categorise my spending into: housing, groceries, transport, eating out, subscriptions, utilities, health, and other. Show me monthly totals for each category. Flag anything that has increased by more than 15% over the period. Suggest two specific areas where I could realistically cut back."

Expect: A categorised breakdown with trends highlighted. Watch for: Privacy. Anonymise account numbers before uploading. Use a paid tier.

8. Compare a financial product

"I am comparing [two health insurance policies / home insurance quotes / energy plans]. Here are the details of each: [paste the key terms, or upload the documents]. Compare them side by side. Tell me which one is better value for someone in my situation: [describe your situation]. Highlight any differences in excess, exclusions, or conditions that might catch me out."

Expect: A structured comparison that highlights the differences that matter for your situation. Watch for: AI is not a financial adviser. Use this as a starting point, not a final decision.

Travel and Leisure

9. Plan a day out with real constraints

"Plan a [Saturday / day trip / weekend away] for [who is going]. We are based in [location]. Constraints: budget of $[amount], one of us is [dietary requirement], we want to be back by [time], and we prefer [walking / driving / public transport]. Include specific places with names, a rough timeline, and lunch recommendations."

Expect: A specific, actionable itinerary with real places. Watch for: AI sometimes suggests places that have closed or changed hours. Verify opening times for anything critical.

10. Research a destination

"We are planning a [length] trip to [destination] in [month]. [Number] of us travelling. We like [interests] and dislike [dislikes]. Budget is [level]. Give me a day-by-day itinerary with specific accommodation suggestions, transport between locations, and restaurant recommendations. Flag anything we should book in advance."

Expect: A detailed itinerary. For well-known destinations, the recommendations will be solid. Watch for: Prices quoted by AI may be outdated. Always confirm accommodation and transport costs directly.

Health and Ageing

11. Prepare for a medical appointment

"I have a [GP / specialist] appointment about [reason]. Here are my symptoms: [describe them — when they started, what makes them better or worse, severity, any other relevant details]. Help me prepare: (1) a structured summary of my symptoms that covers everything the doctor would want to know, (2) three specific questions I should ask, and (3) anything I should bring or do before the appointment."

Expect: A well-organised summary you can take to the appointment, plus questions you might not have thought to ask. Watch for: AI is not diagnosing you. Follow your doctor's advice, not the AI's.

12. Understand medication or test results

"My doctor has prescribed [medication name and dose] for [condition]. Explain in plain English: what it does, common side effects, anything to avoid while taking it, and how long it typically takes to work. Also, I got blood test results showing [specific values]. Are these within normal range? What questions should I ask my doctor about them?"

Expect: A clear explanation. Generally accurate for well-known medications and standard pathology. Watch for: Always follow your doctor's specific instructions. If the AI contradicts your GP, ask your GP.

Learning and Hobbies

13. Get better at something specific

"I am trying to improve my [photography / cooking / gardening / guitar playing / writing / woodworking]. My current level is [beginner / intermediate / experienced but rusty]. The specific thing I am struggling with is [describe it]. Give me a focused practice plan for the next two weeks: what to work on each session, how long each session should be, and one exercise per session. Do not overwhelm me — I can commit to [amount of time] per day."

Expect: A structured, realistic practice plan tailored to your level and available time. Watch for: Nothing specific. This works well across almost any skill.

14. Plan a home project

"I want to [describe the project, e.g. set up a vegetable garden / repaint my living room / organise my garage / build a bookshelf]. The space is [dimensions or upload a photo]. My budget is $[amount] at [Bunnings / local hardware]. I am [skill level] at this kind of work. Give me: a materials list with estimated costs, step-by-step instructions, a realistic time estimate, and any tools I will need. Tell me if any part of this should be done by a professional."

Expect: A practical plan with specific materials, costs, and steps. Watch for: Material prices will be approximate. Check current Bunnings prices before buying.

15. Research family history

"I am researching my family history. I have the following information: [list what you know — names, dates, places, relationships]. Help me: (1) organise this into a structured family tree format, (2) identify the gaps where I am missing information, (3) suggest specific records I should search for (birth/death/marriage certificates, immigration records, census data) and where to find them in Australia, and (4) prepare interview questions for [relative] who remembers [era/events]."

Expect: A structured summary of what you know, clear gaps to fill, and specific research leads. Watch for: AI cannot search genealogy databases for you. It can tell you which databases to search and what to look for.
