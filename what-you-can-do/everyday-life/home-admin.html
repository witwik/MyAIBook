Home Admin and Paperwork

Every household generates paperwork that is tedious to read and important to understand. Body corporate notices, council rate letters, utility contracts, insurance renewal documents, NDIS plans. AI is exceptionally good at turning dense, jargon-filled documents into plain English you can actually act on.

Upload the document (photograph it with your phone if it is on paper) and tell the AI exactly what you need to know.

Understanding official correspondence

“I have uploaded a letter from my body corporate. Summarise it in plain English. Tell me: (1) what they are proposing, (2) what it will cost me, (3) what I need to do and by when, and (4) whether there is anything unusual or concerning I should push back on.”

This works equally well with council notices, strata minutes, lease agreements, and utility contracts. The AI will cut through the legalese and tell you what matters. If the document references legislation or regulations, ask it to explain those too.

Preparing for difficult phone calls

We have all put off a phone call because we did not feel prepared. Insurance disputes, telco billing errors, council complaints, chasing a tradesperson. AI can help you go in ready.

“I need to call my health insurance provider to dispute a claim they rejected. The claim was for a remedial massage that my GP referred me to. The insurer says it is not covered under my Extras policy, but I believe it should be because I have the physiotherapy and natural therapies category. Help me prepare: (1) the key points I should make, (2) the specific questions I should ask, (3) what to say if they refuse, and (4) who to escalate to if the first person cannot help. My fund is [name].”

Print the key points or keep them on screen during the call. You will sound more confident and waste less time going in circles.

NDIS and Medicare paperwork

If you or a family member deals with the NDIS, Medicare bulk billing, or private health insurance claims, you know the paperwork can be bewildering. Upload the document and ask AI to explain it in human terms.

“I have uploaded my NDIS plan review letter. Explain in plain English: what funding categories I have, what the total amounts are, what has changed since my last plan, and what I should raise with my support coordinator if anything looks wrong.”

Privacy warning: NDIS plans, Medicare statements, and health documents contain personal information. Use a paid tier for stronger data protections. Consider redacting names and Medicare numbers before uploading, or describe the situation in general terms instead of uploading the document.

Household budgeting

AI can help you make sense of your spending without needing a dedicated budgeting app. Export your bank statement as a CSV or PDF, anonymise it if you are cautious (remove account numbers and rename payees if you prefer), and upload it.

“I have uploaded three months of bank statements. Categorise my spending into: housing, groceries, transport, eating out, subscriptions, utilities, health, and other. Show me the monthly totals for each category and flag anything that has increased significantly. I want to find where I am spending more than I realise.”

Privacy warning: Bank statements are sensitive. Use a paid plan. If you are uncomfortable uploading statements directly, manually enter the category totals instead and ask AI to analyse the patterns. The analysis is still useful without the raw data.

Try this right now (free)

Find the most recent official letter you have received — a council notice, an insurance renewal, a body corporate circular, anything with dense language. Photograph it with your phone and upload it to Claude or ChatGPT. Ask: “Summarise this in plain English. What do I need to do, and by when?” You will get a clearer answer in 30 seconds than you would from reading it three times.
